# Portfolio
A portfolio of projects spanning embedded electronics and mechanical prototyping

## Vintage Motorcycle Powertrain Conversion
Developed powertrain requirements to build an electric motorcycle focusing on battery, motor, and motor controller specifications using simulations, bench testing, and road testing.

### Requirements & Constraints
This project was born out of necessity. I needed a way to get to my morning classes at a nearby community college so the route and distance of that commute shaped most of the constraints around motor power and battery size and range. Cost was another constraint. While I could buy an electric motorcycle new, the cost at the time was around $14,000. I wanted to do my conversion for less than half that. I needed to create a bike that could go at least 50mph and have a range of about 50 miles of range.

### Testing & Validation
Early validation of component specifications was done through mathematical modeling and simulation software. This aided in right-sizing the three main components of the powertrain: the battery, motor, and motor controller.

Road testing began on an underpowered electric scooter that was modified with a potential motor controller to test the limits of battery and motor output.

The final motor and motor controller were chosen and wired up to a prototype battery. The cost of a battery that satisfied the design requirements was too expensive, so I started to design one that I could manufacture instead. However, manufacturing a custom lithium ion battery proved to be too risky and potentially just as expensive. Also, by this phase of the project, prices for used electric motorcycles dropped to below current-project costs so the project was canceled and a used motorcycle was purchased.
